Branch Avenue DS — Enterprise Appeal Audit
Design system audit · v0.1 → enterprise

The system is disciplined. It isn't yet memorable — or sellable upstairs.

Branch Avenue's foundations are genuinely strong: one pine accent, true-neutral structure, mono numerics, border-defined surfaces. That earns operator trust. What it lacks is what enterprise buyers — dealership principals, HVAC owners, franchise ops, agency clients — need to remember you and defend the purchase: a signature visual asset, a proof layer, compliance signals, and reporting their bosses can read. Six findings below, each with a proposed fix rendered in the system itself.

F5

One voice for four very different buyers

The content rules cover the operator console only. But a dealership GM, an HVAC owner, a franchise director, and an agency founder don't share a vocabulary. Proposal: keep the calm register, add a lexicon per vertical — the same claim, said in their words:

Dealership principal / GM
"Every rooftop on one pane of glass — leads, gross, and inventory by store, by month."
Their units: rooftops, gross, PVR, floor traffic. Never "users" or "conversion events."
HVAC / home-services owner
"Quotes go out while your crews are still on the job. No evening paperwork."
Their units: booked jobs, trucks, crews, callbacks. Respect that they distrust software salesmen.
Franchise / multi-location ops
"Corporate sees the whole system. Each location only sees their own."
Their units: locations, compliance, rollout waves. Lead with control and consistency, not innovation.
Agency & Shopify clients
"White-label reports your clients actually forward to their boss."
Their units: clients, retainers, churn. They're reselling you — arm them to look good.
DS changes: a voice.md lexicon table per vertical, wired into the content-fundamentals section.

F7

Disciplined slid into sterile — the system has no warmth

Everything above makes the system more credible. None of it makes it feel cared for. The brand promise — consistent, approachable, in control, agentic, helpful, understanding — should be felt in the pixels, not just claimed. Three dials, no costume. They compose; you can turn all three.

Dial A · It's alive — agentic presence

Show the system working while you weren't looking.

The branch line draws itself in. Agent dots breathe (the one sanctioned loop). And the first thing you read every morning is what was handled for you — control and care in one sentence. Motion budget stays tiny: draw-in once, breathe on live dots, pop on approve. Nothing else moves.

Dial B · Warm the instrument — surface & color

Tint the neutrals toward pine; let marketing breathe on a warm canvas.

True-neutral gray is why it feels like a spreadsheet. A 2–3% pine tint in the ramp (imperceptible alone, unmistakable in aggregate) plus a warm-sage marketing canvas makes the same layouts feel tended, not clinical. Console keeps today's neutrals; marketing and reports get the warm set.
